# Atheism (Non-Religion/Worldview)

**Overview:**
Atheism in ZooCity is not a religion in the traditional sense, but it plays a major role in the city’s spiritual and social landscape. Atheists reject belief in the supernatural, instead placing their faith in science, reason, and the material world. The practice of Atheism often includes vocal criticism of organized religion, especially the faiths individuals have left behind.

## Demographics & Social Spread
- Atheists come from every species, ethnicity, and background.
- The largest and fastest-growing worldview in ZooCity; may be the majority in some districts.
- Many are focused on everyday concerns (money, power, pleasure), but a vocal minority are "zealots" who actively campaign against religious practices.

## Core Beliefs & Practices
- Rejection of supernatural explanations; emphasis on science, logic, and evidence.
- Some Atheists are indifferent to religion, while others are outspoken critics.
- Many define themselves by what they are not, often focusing on the faith they left.

## Social Frictions & Issues
- **Religious Critique:** Vocal Atheists may provoke or offend religious communities.
- **Community & Meaning:** Some struggle to find community or purpose outside traditional faiths.
- **Materialism:** Critics claim Atheism can lead to excessive focus on wealth, power, or pleasure.

## Plot Hooks
- A scientific society campaigns to ban public religious displays, sparking citywide debate.
- A former religious leader becomes a famous Atheist influencer—are they helping or hurting the city?
- Atheist and religious characters must work together to solve a crisis that challenges both worldviews.

**Note:** Atheism is included here to reflect its real-world social impact and to provide story opportunities about belief, doubt, and coexistence.
